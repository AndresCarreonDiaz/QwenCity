01

What a spectator actually does

The unit is a live life, not a finished show — and the chat is the plot. Four things a viewer can do, in rising order of magic:

WATCH · ~$0
A stylized 2D town (Stardew-ish) where sprite characters live 24/7. A side thought-ticker prints each new memory as it's written — color-coded by type, sized by importance. You are literally watching the mind. "Skip to next notable event" jumps a judge straight to drama.
FOLLOW
Each character has a followable social account and autonomously posts in its own voice whenever it lives something salient (importance ≥ 6), capped a few times per sim-day.
INTERACT
You reply to a post. Your reply is moderated, turned into a memory ("@user replied: …"), given a salience boost, and dropped into the character's stream — where it competes in the next planning + reflection cycle and changes what they do next. Tomorrow they reply back, citing you.
SCREEN
A "▶ what are they living right now?" button compiles the current scene into an 8–20s captioned clip in the character's cloned voice (cheap). A "make it cinematic 🎬" upgrade re-renders the hero beat via Wan. And an auto-produced daily highlight episode catches followers up in 60–90s.
02

How a character keeps its life & memories

Straight from the paper's ConceptNode design: every experience is one natural-language memory with a timestamp, an embedding, and an LLM-scored importance (poignancy, 1–10). To act, the character retrieves only the memories that matter right now. This is the ticker a spectator watches — here's a real slice of one:

◍ maya_okaformemory stream · sim-day 4 · 08:14
dialogueTom said last night he felt ignored at the café opening.7
observeThe bakery down the street has its lights on early.2
inject@judge_02 replied to your post: "you should just call him instead of overthinking it"9
reflectI value the café friendships more than being right about the schedule.8
plan08:30 — walk to Tom's, apologize, then reopen the café together.7
retrieved: top-8 of 1,240next tick: 08:30▲ external reply boosted +2 → surfaced in this plan
observation dialogue reflection plan audience injection

The retrieval score — one function, weighted as the shipped code actually weights it

I(m) = 0.5·recency + 3.0·relevance + 2.0·importance
recency = exponential decay over last-access · relevance = cosine(memory, current situation) via text-embedding-v4 · importance = the stored poignancy ÷ 10. Each component min-max normalized to [0,1]; take the top-8 into the prompt (caps context at ~1–2.5K tokens instead of dumping the whole stream). The audience twist: an ingested human reply gets poignancy +2, so it reliably surfaces in the very next planning and reflection cycle.
03

The one nameable algorithm — the Salience Engine

The 30% Innovation criterion breaks ties, so headline one idea, not a pipeline. Ours: Audience-Coupled Salience Memory — a single importance function I(m) that scores every event on one scale and then drives three subsystems at once. Its elegance is the pitch: one score, three payoffs — and innovation and cost-control turn out to be the same mechanism.

USE 1

Retrieval

Feeds the agent's next-action context — exactly as Stanford did, but now over a stream that includes real human replies as first-class, salience-scored memories.

USE 2

Highlight editing

The day's top-salience events auto-assemble the recap. The memory importance is the edit decision — no separate editor model to build or pay for.

USE 3

Render-budget gating

Only the single highest-salience beat earns an expensive Wan clip; everything else stays cheap HyperFrames. Spend follows drama automatically.

Novelty vs. the seminal paper, in one line: Stanford's town was a closed sandbox; ours is an open, perturbable society — real replies enter the same memory economy and causally rewrite agent trajectories. That closed-vs-open distinction is a genuine research delta, not a reskin — but it only scores if you prove it with an ablation chart (see §07). The novelty lives in the eval, not the vibe.

04

The money shot — audience in the loop

This is the causal beat no competitor — and not even Stanford — can show. It's also the answer to the hackathon's most annoying rule (demo must stay live through Aug 11): the interaction loop turns that burden into the feature.

DAY T · evening
Characters post to their feed when they live something salient (importance ≥ 6), with an avatar-consistent image.
OVERNIGHT
Users and judges reply to the posts.
DAY T+1 · AM
A moderation gate (profanity / prompt-injection / PII + per-user rate limit) filters replies → accepted ones become observation memories with a +2 salience bias → the morning plan is guaranteed to retrieve at least one fresh external observation.
DAY T+1
Reflection may synthesize a new higher-order belief → alters the plan → the character posts back citing @user and appears in that day's highlight, in its cloned voice. The judge sees their own message caused this.
Most clippable moment: "The judge tweeted; the character answered." Live sim → identity-consistent media → cloned voice → human-in-the-loop behavior change → auto-produced episode, in one unbroken take.
05

Why 24/7 for three weeks is affordable

The linchpin decision: do not run in real time. Pre-generate the sim in fast-forward into a buffer that stays ~1 day ahead of a 1× "live" playhead; spectators watch the buffer as if live. Consequence — 100% of generation is offline, so the Batch API's 50% discount applies to every call and there's no latency SLA. That single move, stacked with event-driven ticks and tiered model routing, is a 15–40× cost reduction.

Biggest swing variable is smart-model dialogue turns (~53% of spend) — cap conversations at ~6 turns and keep them on qwen-plus, not max. Cost stops being a risk and becomes a credibility asset: "it's been live since Jul 21 — open it and talk to a character right now."

Judge-safety & never-goes-dark

Spectator view is pure DB/OSS reads → unlimited judges cost ~nothing. A ?mode=replay golden-run auto-engages on a stale heartbeat. All Wan/CosyVoice disabled from user-triggered paths (pre-rendered only); judge chats routed to qwen-flash with per-IP limits + a global daily cap + a read-only kill-switch.

Not Function Compute: it's stateless and its trial credits lapse around Aug 11 — both fight the "continuously running world" premise. A single always-on ECS box is the pragmatic solo pick.

08

The 3-minute demo

Structure one causal money shot, not a feature tour. Name Smallville, AI Town, and Fable in the first minute to preempt the "clone" reflex — then show the beat they can't.

0:00–0:20
It's alive, right now.

Live 2D town, real wall-clock timestamp on screen, thought-ticker scrolling. "This is Maya. Nothing here is scripted. Right now she's deciding whether to apologize to Tom after last night's argument."

0:20–0:50
The society.

Pull back to the relationship graph and an emergent event in progress — a rumor, a party forming. Overlay: "info diffusion: 6/15 agents aware." Establishes multi-agent, not a chatbot.

0:50–1:35
▶ THE MONEY SHOT — audience in the loop.

Type a live reply to Maya's post → it drops into her memory stream with a visible salience score → her next action changes and she cites your message in her reasoning. "That was a real message from outside the simulation, and it just changed what she does next."

1:35–2:10
One mechanism, three payoffs.

The same score assembles today's highlight reel from top-salience events; the single hottest beat renders as a Wan clip with HyperFrames captions in Maya's cloned voice. "One importance function decides what she remembers, what the show highlights, and what we spend GPU on."

2:10–2:40
The measurable claim (wins the track).

Cut to the ablation chart: full multi-agent + audience vs. ablated baselines on info-diffusion speed, relationship-graph density, and audience-causal divergence — the metric Stanford could never report. That's the 30%+30% proof.

2:40–3:00
It's been live the whole time.

"This has run continuously on Alibaba Cloud since Jul 21 — open it and talk to Maya right now." Close on the live URL + QR. The 24/7 constraint becomes a credibility asset judges verify themselves through Aug 11.
